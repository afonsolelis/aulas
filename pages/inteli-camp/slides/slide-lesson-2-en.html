Before going — preparation

A well-prepared visit pays off three times over

Preparation

🎒 What to bring

  • The Day 1 spec printed or at hand — it's the observation script
  • A short list of assumptions to confirm
  • Paper or phone to take notes and record (with permission)
  • Roles split: who asks, who takes notes, who observes

❓ Questions worth gold

  • How does this process work today, step by step?
  • Which criteria weigh in the decision — and how do they compare?
  • Where is there the most manual work or rework?
  • What data exists and in what format does it arrive?
  • What can't be missing — and what must never happen?

During — what to observe

The real context of the process, out of the classroom

Field

👁️ What to observe

Bring the group's spec. The visit confirms or corrects the Day 1 assumptions against the partner's real process.

  • How the process happens in practice — not on paper
  • Which criteria the team uses and how they weigh against each other
  • What data exists and in what format it arrives
  • What the spec got right and what needs revising

🔎 Focus on the essential

  • Follow the data's path, from start to finish
  • Note exact phrases from those who live the problem
  • Capture concrete examples, not generalizations
  • Mark what surprised you — it's often a hidden requirement

Back in the room — debrief

Turn what you saw into a revised spec

Synthesis

🔄 Group debrief

  1. Gather everyone's notes in one place
  2. Compare with the spec: what was confirmed, what changed
  3. Revise the evaluation criteria with what you heard
  4. Note the new requirements and constraints that emerged

✏️ Update the spec

  • Adjust actors, input, output and constraints to reality
  • Refine the acceptance criteria: how we'll know it works there
  • Keep the evidence — it justifies decisions in the pitch
