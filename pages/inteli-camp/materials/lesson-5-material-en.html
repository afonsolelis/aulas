🎯 Goal of the day

Close the bootcamp by presenting the solution to the panel. The morning is dedicated to alignment and rehearsal; the afternoon, to the presentations. Each group has a 4-minute pitch — using it well is as important as having built a good solution.

🕐 Schedule of the day

Agenda

  1. 09:00 – 09:40 — Presentation alignment.
  2. 09:40 – 12:00 — Final development and rehearsal.
  3. 13:00 – 13:30 — Institutional opening and panel introduction.
  4. 13:30 – 15:30 — Group presentations (4-min pitch each).
  5. 15:30 – 16:00 — Scoring, certificates, and closing.

⚖️ What the panel evaluates

Five criteria

  1. Problem Definition: how well the squad understood, deepened and articulated the business opportunity behind the partner's challenge.
  2. Solution-Problem Coherence: does the built solution really address the diagnosed opportunity? Are the use of AI and the approach justified?
  3. Communication and Pitch: clarity, structure and persuasive power of the squad's presentation before the evaluating panel.
  4. Collaboration and Autonomy: how the squad organized itself, distributed responsibilities and drove the project's development independently.
  5. Azul & Brand Alignment: the solution genuinely contributes to the candidate evaluation process, reflecting the tone and values of Azul.

🚫 Mistakes that compromise the evaluation

Avoid

  • A demo with no plan B, dependent on an unstable connection.
  • Reading the slide with your back to the panel.
  • No one able to answer "who uses this?".
  • Going over time — rehearse with a stopwatch.
  • Demonstrating with generic examples instead of the partner's real data.

🏁 Deliverable

Deliverable of the day A 4-minute pitch presented to the panel, with a live demo of the working solution and a demonstration that the output meets the acceptance criteria defined in the spec.
