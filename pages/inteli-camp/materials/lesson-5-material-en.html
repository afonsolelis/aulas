🎯 Goal of the day

Close the bootcamp by presenting the solution to the panel. The morning is dedicated to alignment and rehearsal; the afternoon, to the presentations. Each group has 10 minutes — using them well is as important as having built a good solution.

🕐 Schedule of the day

Agenda

  1. 09:00 – 09:40 — Presentation alignment.
  2. 09:40 – 12:00 — Final development and rehearsal.
  3. 13:00 – 13:30 — Institutional opening and panel introduction.
  4. 13:30 – 15:30 — Group presentations (10 min each).
  5. 15:30 – 16:00 — Scoring, certificates, and closing.

📝 Pitch script — 10 minutes

Copy this four-part structure and fill it in with the group's case. It ensures a coherent narrative from the problem to the solution.

TimePartWhat to show
2'Problem and contextWhich partner pain the solution solves; who suffers from it today. One sentence, no technical jargon.
3'Live demoThe solution working with real data, from input to output. Have a plan B (video or screenshots) in case the internet goes down.
3'How the prompt worksThe logic behind its construction; which parameters control the behavior; how the spec's constraints were ensured.
2'Result vs. criteriaRevisit the spec's acceptance criteria and show that the output meets them. Mention how the group mitigated bias in screening and complied with LGPD. Close with the value delivered to the partner.

🧭 Model script — Azul Linhas Aéreas case

A filled-in example to illustrate the script. Use it as a reference to adapt to the group's work, not as text to copy verbatim.

PartFilled-in example
Problem and contextThe recruiters and Azul HR team responsible for candidate selection face slow, manual screening of interviews, with a high volume of candidates per role and a risk of inconsistent evaluations.
Live demoThe agent takes the interview data and returns the classification and summary of candidates across the stages of the selection funnel, from input (interview) to output (funnel).
How the prompt worksEvaluation criteria parameterized from the role profile, structured output, and constraints against the use of sensitive candidate data.
Result vs. criteriaSelection funnel filled in, meeting the spec's acceptance criteria; bias in screening mitigated and interview data handled in compliance with LGPD.

⚖️ What the panel evaluates

Five criteria

  1. Understanding of the problem: can the group explain the problem in one sentence, without jargon?
  2. Functional solution: does the demo work? Does the output make sense and meet the acceptance criteria?
  3. Prompt quality: does the group justify why the prompt was built that way?
  4. Presentation clarity: is the narrative coherent and does each member know how to talk about what they built?
  5. Responsible AI: does the group explain how it mitigated bias in candidate screening and how the solution complies with LGPD when handling interview data?

🚫 Mistakes that compromise the evaluation

Avoid

  • A demo with no plan B, dependent on an unstable connection.
  • Reading the slide with your back to the panel.
  • No one able to answer "who uses this?".
  • Going over time — rehearse with a stopwatch.
  • Demonstrating with generic examples instead of the partner's real data.

🏁 Deliverable

Deliverable of the day A 10-minute pitch presented to the panel, with a live demo of the working solution and a demonstration that the output meets the acceptance criteria defined in the spec.
