Day 2 — Prompts from the Model

Turn the spec into precise instructions for the AI
Slides ← Back

🎯 Goal of the day

Convert the spec validated on Day 1 into a structured prompt and test it until the output meets the acceptance criteria. The prompt is not a loose piece of text: it is the direct translation of the model into instructions the AI understands and obeys.

🔧 Anatomy of a good prompt

An effective prompt has five parts. Each one corresponds to an element you already defined in the spec.

The 5 parts of an effective prompt

  1. Role: who the AI is in this context — expert, assistant, reviewer.
  2. Context: what the AI needs to know about the problem and the partner.
  3. Task: exactly what it should do — a clear verb, no ambiguity.
  4. Parameters: the variables that change with each use (user input, settings).
  5. Constraints: what it cannot do — derived directly from the spec.

📋 From the spec to the prompt

Consider the example spec from Day 1 (actor: marketing analyst; input: briefing; output: 3 copy variations; constraint: no prohibited words, maximum 280 characters). It translates like this:

You are a copywriter specialized in [partner's sector].
Based on the briefing: [briefing],
create 3 copy variations for social media.
Rules: maximum 280 characters per variation, without using [prohibited words],
keeping the tone of voice [tone].

The parts in brackets are the parameters: what changes with each use. In Lovable, each parameter becomes a form field that the user fills in.

🔁 How to iterate the prompt until it works

A prompt is rarely good on the first version — and that is fine. What sets an efficient group apart is its iteration method.

The iteration cycle

  1. Write the first version with the five parts.
  2. Test with at least three different inputs.
  3. Compare the output against the spec's acceptance criteria.
  4. Identify which part of the prompt caused the deviation.
  5. Adjust only that part and test again.

Most common mistakes

  • Vague prompt: "write something about the product" — the AI makes things up.
  • Missing context: the AI does not know who the partner or the audience is.
  • No constraints: the AI ignores tone and format limits.
  • Changing several parts at once: makes it impossible to know what worked.
