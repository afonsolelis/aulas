Agile Project Management: Fundamentals and Practical Applications

Lesson Goal

Understand the foundations of agile project management, compare Scrum and Kanban, and identify practical tools and metrics that help software teams deliver value iteratively.

1) Agile Manifesto

The Agile Manifesto emphasizes individuals and interactions, working software, customer collaboration, and responsiveness to change. The purpose is not to remove process entirely, but to privilege value delivery and adaptability over rigid control.

2) Scrum

Scrum organizes work in time-boxed sprints. It defines roles such as Product Owner, Scrum Master, and Development Team, and relies on ceremonies like Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, and Retrospective.

3) Kanban

Kanban visualizes flow and controls work in progress. It is particularly useful when work arrives continuously and the team needs to optimize throughput and reduce context switching.

4) Tools and Metrics

Common tools include GitHub Projects, Jira, Trello, and Linear. Common metrics include velocity, lead time, cycle time, and burn-down trends. Good agile management depends on transparency, not only on ceremonies.
